<p><p>White Light Interferometry (WLI) is a precise optical measurement technique used to measure surface topography with nanometer-level accuracy. It employs white light as a source, utilizing interference patterns to measure the phase difference between light waves reflecting off a sample surface and a reference surface. WLI is widely used in applications such as semiconductor manufacturing, materials science, and optical components testing due to its non-destructive nature and high-resolution capabilities.</p><p>The White Light Interferometry (WLI) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.7% during the forecast period.
