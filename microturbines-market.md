<p><p>The microturbine market comprises two primary types: unrecuperated and recuperated microturbines. Unrecuperated microturbines operate without a heat recovery system, delivering lower efficiencies but offering advantages like simplicity and compact design, making them suitable for smaller applications. In contrast, recuperated microturbines incorporate a heat exchanger that captures waste heat, enhancing overall efficiency and enabling higher power output. These systems are ideal for larger-scale applications where maximizing fuel utilization is crucial, leading to reduced operational costs and lower emissions.</p></p>
