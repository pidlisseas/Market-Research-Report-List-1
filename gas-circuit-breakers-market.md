<p><p>Gas circuit breakers (GCBs) are categorized based on their voltage levels: Medium Voltage (MV), High Voltage (HV), Extra High Voltage (EHV), and Ultra High Voltage (UHV). MV GCBs operate typically up to 35 kV, primarily used in industrial applications. HV GCBs handle voltages between 35 kV and 230 kV, suitable for substations. EHV GCBs cover 230 kV to 800 kV, crucial for long-distance transmission. UHV GCBs exceed 800 kV, enabling efficient management of vast power grids and enhancing system reliability.</p></p>
