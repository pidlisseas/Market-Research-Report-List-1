<p><p>The Knee Ankle Foot Orthoses (KAFO) market consists of two primary types: dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to facilitate movement and support rehabilitation by allowing some degree of motion, making them suitable for patients recovering from injuries or surgeries. In contrast, static orthotics provide rigid support and stabilization to immobilize joints, often used for patients with severe conditions or disabilities requiring maximum control over movement. Both types aim to enhance mobility and improve the quality of life for users.</p></p>
