<p><p>RNA-based therapeutics and vaccines focus on harnessing ribonucleic acid (RNA) to treat diseases or prevent infections. RNA-based therapeutics include messenger RNA (mRNA) therapies that guide protein production for conditions like cancer and genetic disorders, and small interfering RNA (siRNA) that silence specific genes to inhibit disease progression. RNA-based vaccines utilize mRNA to instruct cells to produce viral proteins, triggering an immune response without using live pathogens. Together, these approaches represent innovative methods in personalized medicine and infectious disease prevention.</p></p>
