<p><p>Porous concrete, also known as permeable or pervious concrete, is a sustainable construction material characterized by its high void content, allowing water to infiltrate through its structure. This innovative concrete type is primarily used for stormwater management, reducing runoff and improving groundwater recharge. Its applications include driveways, parking lots, sidewalks, and other surfaces where water drainage is essential.</p><p>The Porous Concrete Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing environmental concerns and stringent regulations on stormwater management. Urbanization and the associated rise in impervious surfaces have heightened the need for effective drainage solutions, further fueling market demand. Additionally, advancements in concrete technology and the development of new mixtures enhance the performance characteristics of porous concrete, making it an attractive choice for both residential and commercial projects. </p><p>Latest trends include the integration of recycled materials in porous concrete formulations, which supports sustainable construction practices.
