<p><p>Membrane separation technology encompasses various processes that filter substances based on size and molecular weight. Reverse osmosis employs high pressure to separate ions and small molecules from water, ensuring high purity. Ultrafiltration utilizes membranes to retain larger molecules and colloids, effectively removing bacteria and viruses. Nanofiltration targets smaller particles and divalent ions, while microfiltration is used for larger particles and microorganisms. Other market types may include innovative techniques like pervaporation and membrane distillation, catering to specialized separation applications.</p></p>
