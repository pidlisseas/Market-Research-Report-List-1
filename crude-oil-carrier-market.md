<p><p>The crude oil carrier market is divided into distinct types based on tanker size and cargo capacity. Ultra Large Crude Carriers (ULCCs) exceed 320,000 DWT and are efficient for long-haul transport. Very Large Crude Carriers (VLCCs) range from 200,000 to 320,000 DWT, serving major trade routes. Suezmax tankers, with capacities up to 200,000 DWT, can transit the Suez Canal. Aframax tankers (80,000 to 120,000 DWT) are suitable for shorter routes. Panamax tankers (up to 80,000 DWT) fit Panama Canal dimensions, while small and medium tankers are under 80,000 DWT, used for regional shipping and niche markets.</p></p>
